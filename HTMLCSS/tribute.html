Roger Federer

The Swiss Maestro

With 20 grand slam titles and counting, Roger Federer is considered one of the greatest tennis players of all time.

Some of Roger's most notable achievements:

  • 20 men's Grand Slam singles titles
  • 31 men's Grand Slam finals
  • All 4 Grand Slam finals in 1 season reached three times (2006–2007, 2009)
  • Career Grand Slam
  • 24 consecutive tournament finals won
  • 24 consecutive match wins against top ten opponents
  • 237 consecutive weeks as world No. 1
  • 3 consecutive calendar years as wire-to-wire No. 1 (2005–2007)
  • Oldest No. 1 in ATP history (36 years, 320 days)
  • 15 years ended inside the top 3 (2003–2012, 2014–2015, 2017–2019)
  • 18 years ended inside the top 10 (2002–2015, 2017–2020)
"He’s the most gifted player that I’ve ever seen in my life. I’ve seen a lot of people play. I’ve seen the (Rod) Lavers, I played against some of the great players – the Samprases, Beckers, Connors’, Borgs, you name it. This guy could be the greatest of all time. That, to me, says it all.”
-- Winner of 7 Grand Slams John McEnroe
